Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension.

One time while co-piloting a plane with someone I was at flying school with. We were on an approach in cloud and he was getting low. It seemed as though he had tunnel vision on something at that particulr time and needed to be refocused on other aspects to the phase of flight. The resistance came when I gave my standard call outs of him being low, but there was no adjustment.


Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I tried the standard call outs to make him aware that he was getting low, but no adjustments were made. I had to snap him out of it with plain clear language that he was getting low on approach and he needed to raise the nose and put power in. There are other strategies that I didn't use, but could have if things progressed in to a dangerous state, such as taking over control of the plane, or initiating a go-around. Luckily it never progressed that far.
