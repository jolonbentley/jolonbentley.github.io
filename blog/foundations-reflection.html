Time to reflect on the foundations journey


What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

Firstly I have learnt that no matter how hard I try to not have an ego, it'll always be there. I've learnt that with age or maybe just life experience, I'm becoming more stubborn and set in my ways. Lastly I've learnt that I enjoy reflecting, but not routinely.


What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I guess your values help you make ethical decisions when it comes to what you do, how you approach others and your environment. Empathy will help when it comes to working with and for others. Self awareness will help with guiding your emotions, keeping an open mind, and continual self improvememnt within the industry.


What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

How closed minded I can be when it comes to this stuff. As much as I don't like doing it, I do it because I don't know best. I guess that's the good thing about being self aware, knowing that even though my mind automatically wants to reject it, that doesn't mean I should.


What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The amount of it there is.


Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Various reasons, but most probably to help prepare a good mind for the workplace, collaboration, and problem solving.


Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don't feel like it was a waste of time. I also think depending on where you're at in your life it could be more valuable than I may realise. I do however feel as though it was too much for me and I personally would have preferred spending time doing more programming stuff.
