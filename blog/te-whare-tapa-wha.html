Te Whare Tapa Wha

The four dimensions of wellbeing


Taha Tina (Physical Wellbeing)

In order to help improve and maintain my physical wellbeing, I have been and will continue to run 3km first thing in the morning, four times a week. I've also started trying to eat less carbs and more protein with the foods I consume.


Taha Whanau (Family Wellbeing)

Improving my family wellbeing by starting every morning, and ending each day positively with the relationships within my family. This will be achieved by simple "good mornings" and "good nights", as well as maybe showing affection in some way.


Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

Reflecting each day as I settle (after putting the kids to bed). Spending that quiet time to check in with myself, see what I did well, see where I need to improve, then moving on and knowing that each day comes with new opportunities to live a fulfilled life.


Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

I mentioned reflecting in the previous point to help my emotional and mental wellbeing, but it's also going to contribute to strengthening my spiritual wellbeing also. Given I have a relationship with God, praying and speaking to him helps me bring perspective to my life, as well as make feel more spiritually connected to him, life, and the people around me.


Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

I've been planting plants over the last year or two. These include avocado trees, a feijoa tree, a lemon tree, a mandarin tree, a lime tree, and a passionfruit vine. Caring for them, watching them grow, and seeing some of them bear fruits already makes me feel good and connected to them and their development.
